# Wind Turbine Specs

Technical database of major wind turbine models with specifications, comparisons, and suitability analysis.

## Instructions

When the user asks about wind turbines, turbine specifications, or turbine comparisons:

1. Reference the built-in turbine database below.
2. Provide specifications in a clear table format.
3. If comparing turbines, highlight key differences in rated power, rotor diameter, hub height, and cut-in/cut-out speeds.
4. Provide suitability recommendations based on:
   - Wind class (IEC I, II, III)
   - Site conditions (onshore, offshore, complex terrain)
   - Average wind speed at hub height
5. Include capacity factor estimates where relevant.

## Examples

**User:** Compare Vestas V150 vs Siemens SG 5.8

**Assistant:** Here's a comparison of the Vestas V150-4.2 MW and Siemens Gamesa SG 5.8-170:

| Specification | Vestas V150-4.2 | SG 5.8-170 |
|--------------|-----------------|------------|
| Rated Power | 4.2 MW | 5.8 MW |
| Rotor Diameter | 150 m | 170 m |
| Swept Area | 17,671 m² | 22,698 m² |
| Specific Power | 238 W/m² | 256 W/m² |
| Hub Height Range | 105–175 m | 100–165 m |
| Cut-in Speed | 3 m/s | 3 m/s |
| IEC Class | IIIA | IIA / IIIA |

**Key Differences:**
- The SG 5.8-170 has **38% more rated power** and **28% more swept area**, making it significantly more productive per turbine.
- The SG 5.8-170's higher specific power (256 vs 238 W/m²) means it's optimized for slightly higher wind speeds.
- The Vestas V150 is a pure IEC Class III machine, ideal for low-wind sites (6–7 m/s annual average).
- For a site with 7+ m/s average wind speed, the SG 5.8-170 would deliver substantially more energy per turbine, reducing the number of units needed.
